REDISA hard at work

REDISA has been making inroads in the past months since operations began. In just a few months, over 16 779 tons of waste tyres have been processed and nine processors have been contracted from across the country to turn waste into worth.

REDISA has also contracted five transporters who have furiously collected 16 779 tons of tyres from 70 tyre dealers.

And most importantly, through the programme, 130 people have been employed - who work hard to ensure that South Africa is rid of all tyre waste.

The REDISA Roadshow

Tyre dealers and suppliers from across Johannesburg gathered at the Holiday Inn in Sandton, Johannesburg, for the first of several REDISA supplier events that took place between 9 and 10 October.

In the three months that we have been operational, we have been working hard to rid the country of tyre waste. And not only are we cleaning South Africa, but we have also committed ourselves to turn waste into worth and create 10 000

Braam Malherbe, a presenter from the show 50/50, also spoke at the event about his efforts to highlight the dangers of global warming though the extreme adventures he has undertaken to the South Pole and Great Wall of China.

4th Waste Management Officers Khoro

A REDISA delegation attended the 4th Waste Management Officers Khoro ( meaning "conference" in Venda) that took place in Durban from 14-16 October.

The conference was themed "Making the Management of Waste a Collective Effort" and brought together all government institutions dealing with waste to share information and exchange thoughts on waste management.

"Government cannot address waste management challenges on its own but need a collective effort. The government, private sector, to play. The government, private sector, civil society and the general public have a role to play. Deputy Minister of Water and Environmental Affairs, Rejoice Mabudafhasi.

REDISA Director, Charlie Kirk, attended the conference and said , "it was great to see how the Department of Environment Affairs is working hard to clean up the country".
